Ha Long Bay

located in Quang Ninh Province, in the northeast of Vietnam. Covering an area of 43,400 ha and including over 1600 islands and islets, forming a spectacular seascape.

Phu Quoc Island

Phu Quoc's 150-kilometer coastline, with gentle waves and turquoise water, makes it Vietnam's most popular destination for water sports.

Son Doong Cave

The mighty Rao Thuong River carved out Son Doong as it eroded away the limestone, forming an enormous tunnel beneath the Truong Son Mountains.

Tam Dao

Tam Dao in Vietnamese means three islands, the reason behind is that it includes three peaks which are high above the cloud, make it like three islands flow in the sky.

Sapa

Sapa town stands at the head of a deep valley of magnificent rice terraces that are still farmed today as they have been for centuries.
